Our Story

Four years, one oven,
zero shortcuts.

Richmo started as a wood-fired cart parked outside a Miriswatta petrol shed. It's still, at heart, the same idea — just with a roof now.

How it started

A cart, a gas cylinder, and a stubborn idea

In 2021, Richmo Fernando started selling pizza out of a converted three-wheeler cart on the Miriswatta road — six pizzas a night, cooked on a portable steel oven, sold to whoever walked past. There was no menu. You got what was hot.

Word moved faster than the cart could. Eighteen months in, the queue was longer than the street had patience for, and the clay oven — the one we still cook on — got built.

What Doesn't Change

Three rules we haven't broken

48-hour dough, no exceptions

Cold-proved for two full days. If we run out, we close the kitchen — we don't rush it.

One oven, wood only

No gas backup. When the wood runs low mid-service, the last few tables know why their pizza took longer.

A short menu, kept honest

Twelve pizzas. Nothing added unless something else is good enough to be cut.
